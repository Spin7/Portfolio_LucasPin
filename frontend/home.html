About Me

Engineer, Researcher & Educator

Based in Asunción, Paraguay · Born 2001 · Paraguayan

I am a Mechatronic Engineer passionate about building intelligent systems that connect perception, computation, and decision-making. My projects combine computer vision, machine learning, robotics, and computational modeling to develop intelligent solutions for real-world engineering challenges.

During my undergraduate studies at the Universidad Nacional de Asunción, I graduated with a GPA of 4.82/5 and received the 2026 Honor Roll Distinction. My thesis, "Application of Deep Learning and Agent-Based Modeling for the Development of an Intelligent Mosquito Trap", integrated deep learning, edge computing, IoT, and agent-based simulation for vector surveillance. Alongside my academic projects, I worked as a Research Assistant at the Laboratory of Computer Science and Applied Engineering, contributing to computer vision, CNN training, edge AI, and complex systems simulation. I also served as a Teaching Assistant for Linear Algebra, supporting undergraduate students and developing educational materials.

Beyond individual technologies, I am interested in understanding how intelligent behavior emerges from the interaction between perception, computation, and the physical world. I enjoy developing computational methods that combine mathematical rigor with practical engineering, with a particular interest in robotics, artificial intelligence, and the modeling of complex systems. I am especially motivated by interdisciplinary projects that bridge theory and real-world implementation.
