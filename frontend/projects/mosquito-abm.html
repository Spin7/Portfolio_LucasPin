Overview

Development of an agent-based model for the spatio-temporal simulation of mosquito populations. The model incorporates sex differentiation and purely local behavioral rules, allowing realistic emergent dynamics without centralized control. Detailed Description

This project develops an agent-based model (ABM) that simulates mosquito populations across space and time. Each agent represents an individual mosquito with properties like sex, age, energy, and state (egg, larva, pupa, adult). The agents interact with their local environment and follow biologically-inspired behavioral rules.

The model incorporates sex differentiation — males and females follow different behavioral patterns for mating, feeding, and reproduction. Purely local rules mean no agent has global knowledge; emergent population dynamics arise from simple local interactions, mimicking real ecological behavior.

This work was presented as a poster and abstract at the CNMAC 2025 conference in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil: "Spatio-Temporal Analysis of Mosquito Populations Through Agent-Based Simulation".
