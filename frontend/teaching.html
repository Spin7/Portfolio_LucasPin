Courses

Linear Algebra

Undergraduate Teaching Assistant FIUNA

Second-semester undergraduate course covering the core foundations of linear algebra: vector spaces, linear transformations, matrix decompositions, eigenvalues and eigenvectors, and inner product spaces. The course emphasizes both rigorous proofs and practical computational applications in engineering contexts.

Taught as Teaching Assistant (Profesor Auxiliar) at the Facultad de Ingeniería — Universidad Nacional de Asunción (FIUNA). Responsible for weekly problem-solving sessions, grading, and preparation of supplementary materials.

Lecture Notes

Lecture 1

Vector Spaces and Subspaces

Introduces the fundamental theory of vector spaces: axioms, common examples, and how to identify subspaces within them.

Open slides

Lecture 2

Linear Combinations, Spanning Sets, Bases & Dimension

Develops the tools to describe a vector space: linear combinations, linear independence, spanning sets, bases, and dimension.

Open slides

Lecture 3

Sum of Subspaces, Coordinates & Isomorphism

Explores subspace sums and direct sums, coordinate representations relative to a basis, and the concept of isomorphism between vector spaces.

Open slides

Lecture 4

Linear Maps

Introduces mappings between sets (injective, surjective, bijective) and formally defines linear maps, their uniqueness from basis images, and classic examples: zero operator, identity, derivative, and reflections.

Open slides

Lecture 5

Image, Kernel & Operations on Linear Maps

Defines image and kernel as subspaces, presents the rank-nullity theorem, classifies maps (injective, surjective, bijective), and covers operations: sum, scalar multiplication, composition, and inverse.

Open slides

Lecture 6

Matrix Representation & Change of Basis

Shows how every linear map is represented by a matrix with respect to chosen bases, links image and kernel to column/null spaces, translates map operations into matrix operations, and introduces the change-of-basis matrix.

Open slides

Lecture 7

Inner Product, Norm & Distance

Introduces inner products in real and complex spaces with axioms and examples (ℝⁿ, function spaces, Gram matrix), then defines norm and distance as derived notions, including the ∞, 1, and Euclidean norms.

Open slides

Lecture 8

Orthogonality & Projection

Defines orthogonality and orthogonal complements, presents orthonormal bases, orthogonal projection onto vectors and subspaces, and the Gram-Schmidt algorithm for constructing an orthogonal basis.

Open slides

Lecture 9

Orthogonal Matrices, Fundamental Spaces & Least Squares

Studies orthogonal and unitary matrices, describes orthogonality between null and column spaces of a matrix and its transpose, and develops the least squares problem via the normal equation — applied to curve fitting and MSE minimization.

Open slides

Lecture 10

Diagonalization, Eigenvalues & Eigenvectors

Introduces similar matrices and diagonalization, the characteristic polynomial and Cayley-Hamilton theorem, eigenvalues, eigenvectors and eigenspaces (algebraic and geometric multiplicity), and a concrete algorithm for diagonalizing a matrix from its eigenvectors.

Open slides

Lecture 11

Orthogonal & Unitary Diagonalization

Extends diagonalization to real symmetric and complex Hermitian matrices, proves eigenvalues are real and eigenvectors from distinct eigenvalues are orthogonal, defines orthogonal (D = PᵀAP) and unitary (D = P*AP) diagonalization, and details the algorithm for orthonormalizing the eigenvector basis.

Open slides

Lecture 12

Stochastic Matrices & Markov Chains

Reviews probability basics (sample space, Kolmogorov axioms) to introduce the Markov property and homogeneous Markov chains, models system evolution with a transition matrix, uses diagonalization to compute matrix powers efficiently, and applies steady-state analysis — illustrated with a social mobility case study.
